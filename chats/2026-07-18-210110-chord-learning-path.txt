     Chord Learning Path — Final Plan

     Context

     The user is a beginner pianist (mic input, no MIDI) who wants a complete,
     structured learning
     path for chords: not just how to play them, but why — how chords are built
     from scales,
     which chords go together, harmonic function, cadences, common progressions,
     seventh chords,
     and practical accompaniment (voicings, voice leading, LH patterns, lead
     sheets). Confirmed
     with the user: it lives in the app (theory on-screen + playable practice +
     "practiced ✓"
     badges), and covers all four theory areas (foundations, progressions &
     function, sevenths
     & beyond, accompaniment craft).

     Structure decision: a dedicated topic-track — src/lib/data/chordPath.ts +
     src/screens/ChordPathScreen.svelte at route /chord-path — NOT a guide
     stage-6. The guide
     is grade-staged; this is topic-staged and spans grades. Appending a stage
     would also break
     GuideScreen's hard-coded voice reply ("Stages go from one to five.",
     GuideScreen.svelte:66)
     and cram 7 units into one collapsible. The new module reuses the guide's
     types
     (GuideLink, TheorySection, ExternalResource), guideHref, guideItemStats
     badges,
     GlossText prose rendering, and the GuideScreen rendering idiom.

     The curriculum — 7 units (CHORD_UNITS: ChordUnit[])

     links (all
     badge-able) + check quizzes + moveOnWhen + resources. Existing content is
     deep-linked;
     new lessons are generated (~25 lessons, 6 new generator files).
