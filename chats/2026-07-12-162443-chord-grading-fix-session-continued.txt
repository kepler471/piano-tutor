⏺ I've traced the whole chord-grading path (mic → mono tracker + Basic Pitch worker → fuser → `StepMatcher` → LessonPlayer). Your chords almost certainly *are* being heard — but three design interactions specific to the cadence lessons make it look deaf or wrong. Here's the diagnosis, ranked.
  
  ## 1. The lesson tells you to hold the common tone — the grader requires you to re-strike it
  
  The cadence tips say *"Notice the common tone held between chords — keep that finger down."* But grading is onset-based: `StepMatcher` (`src/lib/practice/matcher.ts:92`) only completes a step when **every** midi in it produces a fresh note-on. A held note produces no new onset from either detector — the mono tracker only reports pitch *changes*, and Basic Pitch only fires on onset activations (the worker's `EDGE_SEC` guard at `basicPitch.worker.ts:133` deliberately drops sustained-note re-detections in later windows).
  
  Concretely in the C cadence: I = {C4,E4,G4}, IV = {C4,F4,A4}. If you hold C4 into chord IV as instructed, the IV step sits at 2/3 collected **forever**. Following the pedagogy makes the grader deaf to the chord. (Same with G4 held from V into the final I.)
  
  ## 2. Lookahead-2 skipping turns stray notes into "it skipped my chord"
  
  `LessonPlayer.svelte:54` creates the matcher with `lookahead: 2` — designed for lossy fast *melodic* playing. On cadences it misfires badly because adjacent chords share tones:
  
  - While stuck on IV (see issue 1), any stray **G4** — a ringing harmonic from chord I, or a mono-tracker flicker — matches V via lookahead and marks IV *skipped*.
  - A stray **E4** (still resonating from chord I) matches the final I two steps ahead and skips **both** IV and V in one shot.
  
  So the failure you experience is: play I (works), hold the common tone into IV (stuck), play V — and its first detected note "skips" IV, which renders as a missed/grey chord. That reads exactly as "it can't hear my chords."
  
  ## 3. The mono tracker is being fed chords it can't represent, and its errors count as wrong notes
  
  In the default `mic-fused` mode both detectors forward (`routing.ts:38`). The MPM mono detector is monophonic; on a triad it locks onto whichever periodicity wins — often a chord tone (harmless), but also **the common-period subharmonic** of the mixture (for C3-E3-G3 in the left-hand segment that's around C1–C2, which is inside the accepted MIDI range) or fifth-locks. Every such misdetection hits `matcher.onOnset` and flashes as a *wrong note* even though your chord was correct — that's the "mishearing" you're seeing. The tracker's own doc comment (`monoTracker.ts:113`) acknowledges exactly this failure mode, but in fused mode those events still grade.
  
  ## Why "timing" seems to matter
  
  It doesn't, directly — the matcher is wait-don't-fail with no clock. What you're feeling is **poly lag**: Basic Pitch reports land 0.5–1.5 s behind your playing. If you play the cadence in tempo (the lesson suggests 60 BPM), chord N's poly notes arrive while you're already on chord N+1, feeding the lookahead-skip machinery in issue 2. Pausing ~2 s between chords masks the bugs, which is why it feels timing-sensitive. (The "chord notes land about a second behind" hint in LessonPlayer only renders for `hand === 'both'` segments, so cadences never show it.)
  
  ## What I'd change
  
  1. **Carry held common tones**: in chord lessons, pre-seed each step's `collected` set with the midis shared with the just-completed step (matches the "keep that finger down" pedagogy), or equivalently only require onsets for the *changed* notes.
  2. **Disable lookahead for chord steps** (`lookahead: 0` when any step has `midis.length > 1`) — the skip heuristic is a melody-mode tool and is actively harmful here.
  3. **Don't let mono misdetections grade as wrong in fused mode** — e.g. in fused mode, only count a mono onset as "wrong" if poly corroborates it, or at minimum filter mono onsets that are subharmonically related to the expected chord.
  
  All three are in pure, vitest-covered modules (`matcher.ts`, `fusion.ts`/routing), so they're testable without a mic. Want me to implement them? I'd start with 1 and 2, which I'm confident fix the "skipped/deaf chord" experience, and treat 3 as a follow-up since it touches detection heuristics.
